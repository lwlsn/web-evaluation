Introduction to the Study - Evaluating Models of Affective Response through Listener Perception of Rhythmic Patterns

We would like to invite you to be part of this research about musical pattern and emotion. Participation in the study is optional. If you choose not to take part there won’t be any disadvantages for you and you will hear no more about it.

Please read the following information carefully before you decide to take part; this will tell you why the research is being done and what you will be asked to do if you take part. Please contact me by email elizabeth.wilson@qmul.ac.uk if there is anything unclear or if you would like more information.

This research will look at evaluating listeners’ perception of music: examining affective/emotional response to short musical phrases. You will initially be asked a few demographic questions designed to assess your musical background and engagement levels.

You will be then presented with the test that allows you to play short audio clips. You are asked to listen to all the clips presented for the duration of the listening test. This should be completed using good quality headphones and in a quiet space, free from interruption.

After listening, you will be asked to give an evaluative rating of the perceived emotional content to the audio. To do so, you be asked to place a slider on a 2-dimensional grid and rate the pieces listened to and rate them in terms of how positive/negative they are and how high/low energy they are.
